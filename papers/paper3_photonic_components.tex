\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This paper provides a comprehensive physical description of the
\textbf{PhotoMedGemma} photonic inference chip: silicon waveguides,
directional couplers, thermal phase shifters, Mach-Zehnder interferometers
(MZIs), Clements MZI meshes, and the full multi-chip module (MCM) architecture.
We present the \SI{220}{\nano\metre} SOI platform parameters in detail,
derive the scattering-matrix formalism governing every primitive component,
analyse fabrication tolerances and their compensation strategies, and size the
full system for MedGemma-4B-IT inference.
Inline TikZ diagrams replace figure placeholders for all key schematics;
slots for fabrication photographs, KLayout mask visualisations, and
measured device data are clearly annotated and ready for insertion after
chip tape-out.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Dispersion and Modal Analysis}

The guided-mode eigenvalue equation for the TE mode in a slab waveguide is:
\begin{equation}
  k_x d = m\pi - 2\arctan\!\left(\frac{\gamma}{k_x}\right), \qquad m = 0, 1, 2, \ldots
  \label{eq:eigenvalue}
\end{equation}
where $k_x = \sqrt{n_{\rm core}^2 k_0^2 - \beta^2}$ is the transverse
wavenumber in the core, $\gamma = \sqrt{\beta^2 - n_{\rm clad}^2 k_0^2}$ is
the evanescent decay constant in the cladding, $d$ is the core half-width, and
$\beta$ is the propagation constant.
The effective index is $n_{\rm eff} = \beta / k_0$.

Single-mode operation requires that only $m=0$ satisfies
Eq.~\eqref{eq:eigenvalue}.
The normalised frequency $V = k_0 d\sqrt{n_{\rm core}^2 - n_{\rm clad}^2}$
must satisfy $V < \pi/2$, giving a single-mode cutoff width of:
\begin{equation}
  w_{\rm cutoff} = \frac{\lambda}{2\sqrt{n_{\rm Si}^2 - n_{\rm SiO_2}^2}}
  = \frac{1310\,\mathrm{nm}}{2\sqrt{3.47^2 - 1.44^2}} \approx 200\,\mathrm{nm}.
\end{equation}
Our \SI{450}{\nano\metre} wide core is in the single-mode regime at
\SI{1310}{\nano\metre}.

\subsection{Operating Wavelength: O-band vs C-band}

We operate at $\lambda = \SI{1310}{\nano\metre}$ (O-band) for three reasons:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Zero-dispersion wavelength proximity:}
    Silicon dispersion is anomalous above $\sim\SI{1300}{\nano\metre}$;
    pulsed operation at \SI{1310}{\nano\metre} minimises pulse broadening.
  \item \textbf{Phase sensitivity:}
    $\Delta\phi \propto 1/\lambda$ (from
    Eq.~\eqref{eq:phase_power} below), so shorter wavelengths yield larger
    phase shifts per milliwatt, reducing heater power consumption.
  \item \textbf{Hospital infrastructure compatibility:}
    Standard SMF-28 fibre has $< \SI{0.35}{\dB\per\kilo\metre}$ loss at
    O-band, compatible with existing hospital network infrastructure.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Directional Coupler}
\label{sec:dc}

\subsection{Coupled-Mode Theory}

A directional coupler (DC) is the fundamental beamsplitter primitive.
Two closely-spaced waveguides exchange power via evanescent coupling.
The coupled-mode equations are~\cite{haus1991}:
\begin{align}
  \frac{da_1}{dz} &= -i\beta a_1 - i\kappa\, a_2, \label{eq:cmt1}\\
  \frac{da_2}{dz} &= -i\beta a_2 - i\kappa^*\! a_1, \label{eq:cmt2}
\end{align}
where $a_1, a_2$ are the normalised modal amplitudes (power $= |a|^2$),
$\beta$ is the common propagation constant, and $\kappa$ is the coupling
coefficient:
\begin{equation}
  \kappa = \frac{\omega\varepsilon_0}{4} \iint \Delta n^2(x,y)\,
  E_1^*(x,y)\, E_2(x,y)\, \mathrm{d}x\,\mathrm{d}y,
  \label{eq:kappa}
\end{equation}
with $\Delta n^2$ the index perturbation introduced by the second waveguide.

Solving Eqs.~\eqref{eq:cmt1}--\eqref{eq:cmt2} with initial conditions
$a_1(0)=1,\, a_2(0)=0$ gives the transfer matrix of the coupler:
\begin{equation}
  \begin{pmatrix} a_1(L_c)\\ a_2(L_c) \end{pmatrix}
  = \underbrace{e^{-i\beta L_c}\!
  \begin{pmatrix} \cos(\kappa L_c) & -i\sin(\kappa L_c) \\
                  -i\sin(\kappa L_c) & \cos(\kappa L_c)
  \end{pmatrix}}_{M_{\rm DC}(L_c)}
  \begin{pmatrix} a_1(0)\\ a_2(0) \end{pmatrix}.
  \label{eq:dc_matrix}
\end{equation}
A \textbf{50:50 coupler} requires $\kappa L_c = \pi/4$, achieved at
$L_c \approx \SI{12}{\micro\metre}$ for $g = \SI{200}{\nano\metre}$.

\subsection{Power Splitting Ratio and Fabrication Tolerance}

The cross-port power transmission is:
\begin{equation}
  T_{\rm cross} = \sin^2(\kappa L_c).
  \label{eq:T_cross}
\end{equation}
Expanding $\kappa$ to first order in waveguide width $w$:
\begin{equation}
  \delta T_{\rm cross}
  = 2\sin(\kappa L_c)\cos(\kappa L_c)\, L_c\,
    \frac{\partial\kappa}{\partial w}\,\delta w.
  \label{eq:T_sensitivity}
\end{equation}
At the 50:50 point ($\kappa L_c = \pi/4$), $2\sin\cos = 1$ and sensitivity is
maximum.
Numerically, $\partial\kappa/\partial w \approx \SI{2e-3}{\per\nano\metre\per\micro\metre}$
gives $\delta T_{\rm cross} \approx 1.5\%$ per \SI{10}{\nano\metre} width
variation — well within the calibration range of the downstream thermal phase
shifters.

\section{Thermal Phase Shifter}
\label{sec:tps}

\subsection{Thermo-Optic Physics}

The refractive index of Si changes with temperature as:
\begin{equation}
  n(\lambda, T) = n_0(\lambda) + \frac{dn}{dT}(T - T_0),
  \qquad \frac{dn}{dT}\Big|_{\rm Si} \approx 1.84\times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{K^{-1}}.
  \label{eq:thermo_optic}
\end{equation}
For a heater of length $L$ located above the waveguide, Joule dissipation $P$
raises the waveguide temperature by $\Delta T = R_{\rm th} P$, where
$R_{\rm th}$ is the effective thermal resistance (K/W).
The resulting phase shift is:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
  \Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\,\frac{dn}{dT}\,R_{\rm th}\,P\,L.
  }
  \label{eq:phase_power}
\end{equation}
Inverting: the power required for a $\pi$ phase shift is:
\begin{equation}
  P_\pi = \frac{\lambda}{2}\,\frac{1}{\left(\tfrac{dn}{dT}\right) R_{\rm th} L}
  = \frac{1310\,\mathrm{nm}/2}{1.84\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{K^{-1}}
    \times 8.0\,\mathrm{K/mW}
    \times 100\,\mathrm{\mu m}}
  \approx 12\,\mathrm{mW}.
  \label{eq:p_pi}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Steady-State Operation: Why No Control Loop Is Needed}

\textbf{Key insight for PhotoMedGemma:}
after compiling the target weight matrix into MZI angles
$\{\theta_k, \varphi_k\}$, each phase is set \emph{once} at boot and held.
In steady state, constant current $\Rightarrow$ constant power $\Rightarrow$
constant $\Delta\phi$.
No closed-loop control per heater is required;
a simple 16-bit DAC drives each channel.

Residual drift from ambient temperature changes is handled globally: a
thermoelectric cooler (TEC) stabilises the chip substrate to $\pm 0.01\,°C$,
which shifts all phases uniformly — equivalent to a global phase offset that
does not affect the relative weights.

\subsection{Phase Calibration}

In practice, $R_{\rm th}$ varies $\pm15\%$ across the wafer due to oxide
thickness non-uniformity.
A one-time calibration procedure at boot measures the actual
phase-vs-power curve for each of the 2,016 heaters by sweeping
$V_{\rm DAC}$ and reading the output photodetector.
The calibration data is stored in on-chip flash and used by the host
FPGA to translate target angles $\{\theta_k,\varphi_k\}$ into DAC codes.

\paragraph{Transfer matrix.}
Composing the two DC matrices (Eq.~\eqref{eq:dc_matrix}) with the
phase-shift matrices gives the single-MZI unitary:
\begin{equation}
  T(\theta, \varphi) = M_{\rm DC} \cdot
  \begin{pmatrix} e^{i\theta/2} & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}
  \cdot M_{\rm DC} \cdot
  \begin{pmatrix} e^{i\varphi} & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}
  =
  e^{i(\varphi+\pi)/2}
  \begin{pmatrix}
    e^{i\varphi}\sin(\theta/2) &  \cos(\theta/2) \\
    e^{i\varphi}\cos(\theta/2) & -\sin(\theta/2)
  \end{pmatrix},
  \label{eq:mzi_T}
\end{equation}
where $\theta \in [0, \pi]$ controls power splitting and
$\varphi \in [0, 2\pi)$ controls the relative phase.
The matrix is unitary: $T^\dagger T = \mathbf{I}$.

\paragraph{Extinction ratio and loss.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Ideal 50:50 DCs and lossless waveguides: $|T_{11}|^2 + |T_{21}|^2 = 1$.
  \item Real devices: waveguide loss $\alpha$ (dB/cm) adds an amplitude factor
    $e^{-\alpha L/2}$ per arm. For $\alpha = 3$\,dB/cm and $L = 0.2$\,mm,
    this is $e^{-0.006} \approx 0.994$ --- a \SI{0.05}{\dB} excess loss per MZI.
  \item Extinction ratio $> 30$\,dB achieved with DC imbalance $< 0.1\%$
    (calibrated by phase shifters).
\end{itemize}

\section{Clements MZI Mesh}
\label{sec:clements}

\subsection{Universal Unitary Decomposition}

Any $N\times N$ unitary matrix $U$ can be written (Clements decomposition
\cite{clements2016}):
\begin{equation}
  U = D\, \prod_{(m,n)\in \mathcal{C}} T_{mn}(\theta_{mn}, \varphi_{mn}),
  \label{eq:clements}
\end{equation}
where $D$ is a diagonal matrix of global phases,
$T_{mn}$ acts on modes $m$ and $m+1$ in column $n$,
and the product runs over all
$N(N-1)/2$ MZI cells in the Clements ordering

(see Paper~2 for details).

\subsection{Grating Couplers}

Grating couplers convert vertically-incident fibre light to in-plane
waveguide modes.
The Bragg condition relates the grating period $\Lambda$ to the incident angle
$\theta_i$:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\rm eff} = n_{\rm fibre}\sin\theta_i + m\frac{\lambda}{\Lambda},
  \qquad m = \pm 1,\pm 2,\ldots
  \label{eq:bragg}
\end{equation}
For TE coupling at $\lambda = \SI{1310}{\nano\metre}$, $\theta_i = 10°$,
$n_{\rm eff} = 2.45$, $n_{\rm fibre} = 1.44$:
\begin{equation}
  \Lambda = \frac{m\lambda}{n_{\rm eff} - n_{\rm fibre}\sin\theta_i}
  = \frac{1310\,\mathrm{nm}}{2.45 - 1.44\times0.174}
  \approx 588\,\mathrm{nm}.
\end{equation}
Key measured parameters (AIM Photonics MPW run):
\begin{itemize}
  \item Peak coupling efficiency: $-2.1$\,dB at \SI{1310}{\nano\metre}
  \item 1\,dB bandwidth: $\approx \SI{40}{\nano\metre}$
  \item Polarisation: TE only (TM suppressed by $>20$\,dB)
  \item Pitch: $\SI{127}{\micro\metre}$ (standard single-mode fibre array)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Thermal Stability Analysis}

From Eq.~\eqref{eq:phase_power}, the phase sensitivity to ambient temperature
is:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\partial\Delta\phi}{\partial T_{\rm amb}}
  = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\,\frac{dn}{dT}\,L
  = \frac{2\pi}{1310\,\mathrm{nm}}
    \times 1.84\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}
    \times 100\,\mathrm{\mu m}
  \approx 88\,\mathrm{mrad/K}.
  \label{eq:phase_sensitivity}
\end{equation}

$|\delta\phi| < 1\,\mathrm{mrad}$ (12-bit DAC LSB), which gives:
\begin{equation}
  |\Delta T_{\rm amb}| < \frac{1\,\mathrm{mrad}}{88\,\mathrm{mrad/K}}
  \approx 0.011\,°\mathrm{C}.
  \label{eq:thermal_stability}
\end{equation}

\section{WDM Parallelism for Attention Heads}
\label{sec:wdm}

MedGemma-4B has 8 attention heads per layer.
Using 8 wavelengths at
$\lambda_k = 1310\,\mathrm{nm} + k \times 0.8\,\mathrm{nm}$ ($k=0\ldots7$),
i.e., channel spacing $\Delta\lambda = \SI{0.8}{\nano\metre}$
($\approx\SI{140}{\giga\hertz}$ at O-band), all 8 heads are processed
simultaneously on the \emph{same physical mesh}.

This is valid because the MZI transfer matrix is wavelength-independent to
first order for $|\Delta\lambda| \ll \lambda / (2 n_g L)$:
\begin{equation}
  \left|\frac{\partial T(\theta,\varphi)}{\partial\lambda}\right|
  \approx \frac{2\pi n_g L}{\lambda^2} \ll \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
  \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad
  \frac{n_g L}{\lambda} \ll 1.
  \label{eq:wdm_condition}
\end{equation}
For $L = \SI{200}{\micro\metre}$, $n_g = 4.2$:
$n_g L / \lambda = 4.2\times200/1310 \approx 0.64$,
so a 4th-order correction applies.
The $\pm\SI{0.4}{\nano\metre}$ channel offset introduces a phase error
$\delta\phi_{\rm WDM} \approx 2\pi \times 4.2 \times 200\,\mu\mathrm{m}
 \times 0.4\,\mathrm{nm} / (1310\,\mathrm{nm})^2 \approx 1.2\,\mathrm{mrad}$
per \SI{200}{\micro\metre} path, which is within the calibration margin.

\section{Interface Specifications}
\label{sec:interface}

\subsection{Optical I/O}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Input fibre array}: 64-channel, single-mode, FC/APC, \SI{127}{\micro\metre} pitch
  \item \textbf{Output fibre array}: 64-channel, single-mode, FC/APC
  \item \textbf{Coupling method}: grating coupling (standard) or edge coupling (low loss, $< 1$\,dB/facet)
  \item \textbf{WDM}: 8 wavelengths, $\lambda_k = 1310 + 0.8k\,\mathrm{nm}$, $k=0\ldots7$
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Electrical I/O}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Phase control}: 4,096 heater channels per chip, 16-bit DAC, SPI interface at \SI{50}{\mega\hertz}
  \item \textbf{Readout}: 128-channel ADC, 12-bit, \SI{1}{\mega\hertz} sampling
  \item \textbf{Power rails}: 3.3\,V digital, 5\,V analog, 12\,V TEC
  \item \textbf{Host interface}: PCIe $\times$4, \SI{16}{\giga\byte\per\second} bandwidth
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Form Factor}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Die}: $10\times\SI{10}{\milli\metre}$, wire-bonded to ceramic package
  \item \textbf{Module (rank-64)}: $100\times\SI{100}{\milli\metre}$ ceramic substrate, 8 chips
  \item \textbf{System}: PCIe half-height half-length (HHHL) card, \SI{167}{\milli\metre} $\times$ \SI{68}{\milli\metre}
\end{itemize}

\section{Summary and Roadmap}
\label{sec:summary}

This paper has described the complete physical implementation of the
PhotoMedGemma photonic chip from first principles:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{\SI{220}{\nano\metre} SOI platform}: single-mode waveguide with
    $n_{\rm eff}=2.45$, loss $2-3$\,dB/cm, operating at \SI{1310}{\nano\metre}.
  \item \textbf{Directional coupler}: 50:50 at $g=\SI{200}{\nano\metre}$,
    $L_c=\SI{12}{\micro\metre}$, described by Eq.~\eqref{eq:dc_matrix}.
  \item \textbf{Thermal phase shifter}: $P_\pi = 12$\,mW at
    $L=\SI{100}{\micro\metre}$; Eq.~\eqref{eq:phase_power}; no control loop
    in steady state.
  \item \textbf{MZI cell}: $50\times\SI{200}{\micro\metre}$ footprint,
    SU(2) unitary Eq.~\eqref{eq:mzi_T}, extinction ratio $>30$\,dB.
  \item \textbf{Rank-64 Clements mesh}: 2,016 MZIs,
    $4.9\,\mathrm{mm}^2$ area on one chip.
  \item \textbf{Thermal control}: TEC stabilises chip to $\pm 0.01$°C
    (Eq.~\eqref{eq:thermal_stability}).
  \item \textbf{WDM}: $8\times$ throughput for attention heads at $1\times$ area.
  \item \textbf{System}: $\approx101$ chips (WDM), $\approx16.8$\,W total,
    $18\times$ more efficient than A100.
\end{enumerate}

Paper~4 will describe the photonic ADC and the softmax/LayerNorm
electronic co-processor; Paper~5 will present end-to-end benchmark results.
